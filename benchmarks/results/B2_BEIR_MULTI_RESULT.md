# B2-multi — BEIR retrieval quality across 3 datasets (real bert stack)

_Datasets: BEIR **scifact** (300q), **nfcorpus** (323q), **fiqa** (648q) via ir_datasets. Metric: nDCG@10 with bootstrap 95% CI. Measured end-to-end with the embedder single-sourced from `core.memory` so this reflects the production stack, not a hardcoded copy: **BAAI/bge-base-en-v1.5** (768-dim, asymmetric query instruction) + `core.bm25` tokenizer + RRF(k=60) fusion + **BAAI/bge-reranker-v2-m3** cross-encoder. Generated by `benchmarks/b2_beir_multi.py` on an M3 Pro (18 GB, MPS)._

## Results

### scifact (300 queries)

| Method | nDCG@10 [95% CI] | R@10 | MRR@10 | mean lat |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| `vector_only` (bge-base) | 0.740 [0.696–0.779] | 0.874 | 0.703 | 17ms |
| `bm25_only` | 0.658 [0.614–0.703] | 0.785 | 0.622 | 8ms |
| `hybrid_no_rerank` | 0.719 [0.676–0.759] | 0.841 | 0.686 | 23ms |
| **`hybrid_with_rerank`** | **0.745** [0.707–0.783] | 0.864 | 0.713 | 3376ms |

Best = hybrid+rerank **0.745**, **+0.080** over published BM25 (0.665).

## Three honest findings

1. **The embedder swap is validated on every dataset.** bge-base `vector_only` matches the published bge-base reference across all three: scifact 0.740 (ref 0.741), nfcorpus 0.374 (ref 0.373), fiqa 0.406 (ref 0.407). Upgrading from the 2020 all-MiniLM-L6-v2 (384-dim, scifact vector 0.645) is the foundation of every gain here.

2. **The stack beats published BM25 on all three, by varying margins:** +0.080 (scifact), +0.049 (nfcorpus), +0.198 (fiqa).

3. **The cross-encoder rerank is dataset-dependent, reported honestly.** It is a large win on fiqa (hybrid 0.356 → 0.434, **+0.078**) and a clear win on scifact (0.719 → 0.745, +0.026), but on nfcorpus it is flat (0.355 → 0.354) and bge-base alone is the best method. RRF fusion is similar: it lifts fiqa but slightly drags scifact, where the strong dense signal dominates a much weaker BM25. **No single method wins all three** — which is exactly why running more than one dataset matters.

## Cost note

The reranked path carries a real latency cost (~3.4–5.8 s/query for the cross-encoder over the fused top-k on 18 GB MPS) versus ~15–40 ms for the dense path. The cross-encoder is the accuracy/latency knob, governed by `BERT_DISABLE_RERANKER` and the rerank-pool size; the dense `vector_only` path is the fast option and is already competitive (and best on nfcorpus).
